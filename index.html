What are Microgreens?

Microgreens are the first pair of very young true leaves.

The microgreens are typically harvested at 5-20 days after germination when they measures a few centimeter height.



Health benefits and Nutrition


1. Microgreens are power packed with nutrients:

Most of the microgreens are the good source of Vitamin C, Beta Carotene, Vitamin K and Vitamin E. They are also rich in many minerals viz., Manganese, Folate, Calcium, Magnesium, Potassium, Copper, Zinc etc thus making the microgreens as the power house of nutrients and minerals. It is evident from the research that, microgreens contain 40 times more nutrients than their mature counterparts.

2. Microgreens contain Polyphenols:

Microgreens diets high in polyphenols which are important natural chemicals and contain powerful antioxidant properties which have been associated with a reduced risk of heart disease, cancer and Alzheimer’s disease.

3. Microgreens improve heart health:

Microgreens in diet can reduce the risk of heart diseases. Results emanated from research have shown that, microgreens are known to reduce bad LDL cholesterol and triglycerides thereby lowering the risks of heart diseases.

4. Microgreens reduce chronic disease risk:

Microgreens dietary profile is filled with nutrients and polyphenols which are meant to reduce risk of several types of cancer, including prostate cancer and cancers affecting the digestive tract. Also lowers inflammation and a reduced risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes and obesity.

Dietary Tips

  • Using them as a topping for salads and soups.
  • Tossing a small handful into a smoothie or juice before blending.
  • Using them as a garnish alongside any main dish.
  • Placing microgreens on top of a flatbread or pizza after cooking.
  • Adding microgreens into an omelet or frittata.
  • Replacing lettuce with microgreens on a burger, sandwich, or tacos.



Microgreens can be grown from various seeds. Their taste can vary greatly depending on the variety. Microgreens are rich in nutrients. They often contain larger amounts of vitamins, minerals and antioxidants than their more mature counterparts. Microgreens deliver a concentrated dose of nutrients and beneficial plant compounds. As a result, they may reduce the risk of certain diseases.
